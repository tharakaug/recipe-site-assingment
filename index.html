Sri Lankan Food

Sri Lankan traditional food is known for its particular combinations of herbs, spices, fish, vegetables, rice, and fruits. The cuisine is highly centered around many varieties of rice, as well as coconut, which is a ubiquitous plant throughout the country. Seafood also plays a significant role in the cuisine, whether fresh fish or preserved. As a country that was a hub in the historic oceanic silk road, contact with foreign traders brought new food items and cultural influences, in addition to the local traditions of the country's ethnic groups, all of which have helped shape Sri Lankan cuisine.


01. Kiribath (Milk Rice)

Kiribath is rice cooked in salted coconut milk until the grains turn soft and porridge-like. Generally eaten for breakfast, kiribath is also prepared on special occasions such as birthdays, New Year's, and religious festivals.

02. Hoppers

Hoppers, also known as Appam, are a beloved traditional food in Sri Lanka, especially popular as a breakfast or dinner dish. They are a type of bowl-shaped pancake with crispy edges and a soft, fluffy center, made from a fermented batter of rice flour and coconut milk. Hoppers are a staple in Sri Lankan cuisine and are known for their unique texture and taste.

03. String Hoppers

String hoppers, known as Idiyappam in Tamil, are a popular traditional dish in Sri Lanka, especially enjoyed for breakfast or dinner. They are made from rice flour dough that is pressed through a special mold to create thin, noodle-like strands, which are then steamed into delicate, lacy disks.

04. Rice & Curry

Rice and curry is the quintessential meal in Sri Lanka, forming the backbone of the island’s cuisine. It’s more than just a dish; it's a culinary tradition that showcases the richness and diversity of Sri Lankan flavors. The meal typically consists of a serving of steamed rice accompanied by a variety of curries and side dishes, creating a balanced and flavorful experience.

05. Pittu

Pittu is a traditional Sri Lankan dish that is simple yet deeply satisfying, often enjoyed as a breakfast or dinner meal. It consists of a mixture of rice flour and grated coconut, which is steamed in a cylindrical mold to create a dense, crumbly texture. This dish is popular in both Sri Lanka and South India, and it is cherished for its versatility and ability to pair with a variety of accompaniments.

06. Roti

Roti (also spelled rotti) is a popular and versatile flatbread in Sri Lankan cuisine, commonly enjoyed at any time of the day. It’s a simple, yet satisfying dish that can be served plain, stuffed with fillings, or accompanied by various curries and sambols. Sri Lankan roti has several variations, each with its unique taste and texture.
